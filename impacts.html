Success Stories

Community Garden in Jakarta

Through Greenesia, a group of residents in Jakarta mapped unused vacant lots. These maps became the foundation for community gardens that provide fresh produce while serving as spaces for social interaction and environmental education. With support from the city government, the movement has grown stronger. Today, hundreds of families are actively engaged in urban farming, building food security while creating green spaces in the heart of the city.

Reforestation in Borneo

In Borneo, Greenesia partnered with a local NGO to map critical forest areas affected by deforestation. This data guided reforestation efforts by identifying priority zones for tree planting. The initiative led to the planting of over 10,000 trees in just three months. Beyond restoring ecosystems, it mobilized volunteers, created local job opportunities, and raised public awareness about the importance of protecting forests.

Get Involved

Change starts with small steps. You can make a difference by mapping green spaces in your own neighborhood, joining existing community projects, or simply sharing knowledge with others. Every action, big or small, contributes to a healthier and more sustainable future.
